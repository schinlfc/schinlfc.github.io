Sayorn Chin, Ph.D.

Applied Microeconomist, Data Scientist

I am a faculty member in the Economics Department at Lafayette College in Easton, Pennsylvania. I also hold the position of data science fellow affiliate at Mountain Data Group. My research interests lie in applied microeconomics, particularly at the intersection of health, labor, demographics, and aging. My academic work has been published in popular journals such as Demography, Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization, The Journal of the Economics of Ageing, Public Health Reports, and Journal of Substance Use. My research has been cited by The Wall Street Journal. I investigate the determinants and consequences of health disparities and social inequality. I teach intermediate microeonomics, development economics, and health economics at Lafayette College.

I completed my Ph.D. and M.A. in Economics from Colorado State University and earned my B.A. in Mathematical Economics from Colorado College. I also graduated from the United World College of South East Asia, an independent international school in Singapore, and is a member of the United World College movement which includes 18 schools worldwide.
