Sayorn Chin

Applied Microeconomist, Data Scientist

I am a faculty member in the Economics Department at Lafayette College in Easton, Pennsylvania. I also hold the position of data science fellow affiliate at Mountain Data Group. My reseach interests lie in applied microeconomics, particularly at the intersection of health, labor, demographics, and aging. As an applied microeconomist, I answer policy-relevant questions using modern tools of causal inference techniques from applied statistics and econometrics. I teach intermediate microeonomics, development economics, and health economics at Lafayette College.

I completed my Ph.D. and M.A. in Economics from Colorado State University and earned my B.A. in Mathematical Economics from Colorado College. I also graduated from the United World College of South East Asia, an independent international school in Singapore, and is a member of the United World College movement which includes 18 schools worldwide.
