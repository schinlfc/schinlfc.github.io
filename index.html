Sayorn Chin, Ph.D.

Applied Microeconomist | Data Scientist

I am a faculty member in the Economics Department at Lafayette College in Easton, Pennsylvania. I also hold the position of data science fellow affiliate at Mountain Data Group. My research interests lie in applied microeconomics, particularly at the intersection of health, labor, demographics, and aging. I investigate the determinants and consequences of health disparities and social inequality. My academic work has been published in popular journals such as Demography, Journal of Economic Behavior & Organization, The Journal of the Economics of Ageing, and Public Health Reports, among others. My research has been cited by the Wall Street Journal, CallMatters, and featured in a professional podcast, and sponsored by Bloomberg Philanthropies.

I completed my Ph.D. and M.A. in Economics from Colorado State University and earned my B.A. in Mathematical Economics from Colorado College. I also graduated from the United World College of South East Asia, an independent international school in Singapore, and is a member of the United World College movement which includes 18 schools worldwide.

Outside the office, you’ll often find me watching English Premier League matches—especially Liverpool, since I’m a devoted fan—and looking for pick-up soccer games to join. I also enjoy playing piano and guitar whenever I can. When I’m not doing that, I love going on hikes or long walks with my partner, Alexandra (who also works at Lafayette College), and our tiny puppy, Ziggy (though we often call him Monkey—he’ll always be our little pup). Whenever possible, I bring him along to my office, too.
